1. Introduction & Disclaimer

LEGAL DISCLAIMER: ZO Syndicate RP is a FICTIONAL roleplay server. All activities, including drug dealing, are SIMULATED for entertainment purposes only. This content does NOT condone, encourage, or provide real-world guidance for illegal activities. All players must be 18+ and understand the distinction between roleplay and reality.

Welcome to the underground economy of San Andreas. Drug dealing in ZO Syndicate RP represents one of the most lucrative — and dangerous — career paths available to civilians. Unlike legal employment with the LSPD, EMS, or legitimate businesses, the narcotics trade operates in the shadows, where fortunes are made and lost on street corners, in clandestine labs, and through gang-controlled territories.

1.1 What is Drug Dealing?

Drug dealing is a multi-phase criminal enterprise involving:

  • Production — Growing, processing, or cooking narcotics from raw materials
  • Quality Control — Ensuring product purity to maximize profit margins
  • Distribution — Selling finished product through street corners or gang territories
  • Risk Management — Evading law enforcement heat and rival gang interference

1.2 Risk vs Reward Overview

The narcotics trade offers income potential of $4,000-12,000 per day for experienced dealers, compared to $2,400-3,200 for legal jobs. However, this comes with significant risks:

  • Arrest & Prosecution — Drug charges carry fines up to $50,000 and jail sentences up to 120 months (2 hours real-time)
  • Asset Seizure — Police can confiscate cash, vehicles, and property linked to drug operations
  • Gang Warfare — Rival organizations may raid your labs or territory selling spots
  • Heat System — Repeated illegal activity triggers automatic police alerts
"The street corner is a businessman's office. The difference is, your office might get raided, your clients might be undercover cops, and your competitors carry guns instead of briefcases."

2. Drug Types & Production

ZO Syndicate RP features three tiers of narcotics, each with distinct production methods, profit margins, and risk levels. Progression through the tiers requires gang rank advancement and capital investment.

2.1 Weed (Cannabis)

Tier: Entry-level | Risk Level: Low | Income: $100-200/oz

Production Process

  1. Acquire Seeds — Purchase from black market dealers or gang suppliers ($50/seed pack)
  2. Plant & Water — Use one of 10 grow house locations. Plants require watering every 4 hours (in-game time)
  3. Harvest — Mature plants (24 hours growth) yield 4-6 ounces per plant
  4. Dry & Package — Drying process takes 2 hours. Improper drying reduces quality

Quality Factors

  • Watering Schedule: Consistent watering = higher THC content = higher quality
  • Harvest Timing: Premature harvest (under 20 hours) reduces quality by 30%
  • Drying Time: Full 2-hour dry = high quality; rushed dry (under 1 hour) = low quality
Quality Level Street Price (per oz) Production Requirements
Low Quality $100 Rushed harvest, inconsistent watering, quick dry
Mid Quality $150 Standard 24-hour growth, normal watering, full dry
High Quality $200 Optimal timing, perfect watering, controlled dry, gang stash storage
Beginner Tip: Start with weed to learn the mechanics risk-free. A single grow house can generate $800-1,200 per harvest cycle (24 hours), with minimal police attention until you scale operations.

2.2 Cocaine

Tier: Intermediate | Risk Level: Medium | Income: $300-600/oz

Production Process

  1. Acquire Coca Leaves — Import from cartel contacts or gang suppliers ($120/kg)
  2. Processing — Use one of 5 cocaine labs. Processing minigame requires correct chemical ratios
  3. Cutting — Pure cocaine can be cut with inert powder to increase volume (reduces quality)
  4. Packaging — Brick press or bag packaging (bricks sell for higher margins to bulk buyers)

Quality Factors

  • Chemical Precision: Correct ratios during processing = 90%+ purity
  • Cutting Agent: No cutting = high quality; 50% cut = low quality
  • Lab Cleanliness: Labs must be maintained (cleaned every 10 batches) to prevent contamination
Quality Level Street Price (per oz) Production Requirements
Low Quality $300 Heavy cutting (50%+), imprecise processing, dirty lab
Mid Quality $450 Light cutting (20%), standard processing, maintained lab
High Quality $600 Pure product (no cut), expert processing, pristine lab
Gang Rank Lock: Cocaine production requires Member rank or higher in your gang. Recruit-level players can assist with distribution but cannot access labs.

2.3 Methamphetamine

Tier: Expert | Risk Level: High | Income: $500-1,000/oz

Production Process

  1. Acquire Ingredients — Pseudoephedrine, red phosphorus, anhydrous ammonia ($400/batch from chemical suppliers)
  2. Cooking Minigame — Multi-stage process requiring temperature control and timing precision. Failed cook = explosion (lab damage + heat increase)
  3. Crystallization — Proper cooling phase determines crystal purity
  4. Quality Check — Test kit shows purity percentage (60%-99%)

Quality Factors

  • Temperature Control: Maintain correct temp ranges during cook (95-105°C)
  • Timing: Each cooking stage has optimal timing windows (5-15 seconds leeway)
  • Lab Equipment: Upgraded labs ($50,000 investment) reduce failure rate by 40%
  • Experience: Cook success rate increases with each successful batch (tracked stat)
Quality Level Street Price (per oz) Purity Range Production Requirements
Low Quality $500 60-74% Basic lab, rushed cook, beginner chemist
Mid Quality $750 75-89% Standard lab, careful timing, intermediate chemist
High Quality $1,000 90-99% Upgraded lab, perfect execution, expert chemist (20+ successful cooks)
EXPLOSION RISK: Failed meth cooks trigger lab explosions, resulting in:
  • Loss of all ingredients and product in lab ($5,000-20,000 loss)
  • +30 police heat (instant alert if heat was already above 50)
  • Potential injury requiring EMS (broken bones, burns)
  • Lab unusable for 6 hours (real-time) while repairs are made
Gang Rank Lock: Methamphetamine production requires Lieutenant rank or higher. Only the most trusted gang members gain access to meth labs due to explosion risks and high police priority.

3. Quality System

Product quality is the single most important factor determining profitability. The quality system in ZO Syndicate RP simulates real-world purity standards, where higher-quality narcotics command premium prices but require more skill, time, and investment to produce.

3.1 Quality Tiers Explained

Quality Tier Price Multiplier Street Reputation Impact Buyer Acceptance Rate
Low Quality 50% of base price -5 reputation per sale 60% (buyers reject or negotiate down)
Mid Quality 100% of base price No change 85% (standard acceptance)
High Quality 150% of base price +10 reputation per sale 95% (high demand, premium clients)

3.2 How to Improve Quality

Weed Quality Improvement

  • Consistent Watering: Set alarms for every 4 hours (in-game time). Missed watering = -10% quality
  • Optimal Harvest: Harvest between 24-28 hours of growth for peak THC
  • Controlled Drying: Use gang stash house drying racks (faster + quality boost)
  • Storage: Store in humidity-controlled gang stash (+5% quality retention)

Cocaine Quality Improvement

  • Chemical Precision: Practice the processing minigame in solo sessions to master ratios
  • Pure Product: Avoid cutting unless targeting low-income buyers deliberately
  • Lab Maintenance: Clean labs every 10 batches using cleaning supplies ($500)
  • Equipment Upgrades: Invest in precision scales ($5,000) for exact measurements

Meth Quality Improvement

  • Temperature Mastery: Watch thermometer closely — overheating by 10°C = ruined batch
  • Timing Practice: Each cook stage has a 5-15 second optimal window. Early = impure; late = burnt
  • Lab Upgrades: Upgraded labs ($50,000) provide visual/audio cues for timing windows
  • Experience: Your character gains a hidden "Chemistry" skill (1-100) that improves success rate
  • Partner Cooking: Two chemists in the same lab provide a +10% quality bonus (coordination)
Reputation System: Street reputation (0-1000 scale) unlocks premium buyers who only purchase high-quality product. Reaching 500+ reputation grants access to "VIP Buyers" who pay 200% of base price for guaranteed high-quality goods.

3.3 Quality Testing

Players can purchase Drug Test Kits ($200 each) from black market vendors to test product purity before selling. Testing reveals:

  • Purity Percentage: 60%-99% (displayed as color-coded bar: red = low, yellow = mid, green = high)
  • Cutting Agents: List of detected additives (e.g., "Baking soda detected: 40% by weight")
  • Estimated Street Price: Based on current market demand and quality

4. Street Corner Selling (Active Income)

Street corner selling is the primary active distribution method, requiring player presence and interaction with NPC buyers. This method offers high income potential ($500-1,500/hour) but exposes you to police heat and rival gang confrontations.

4.1 How Street Selling Works

  1. Navigate to a Selling Zone — 15 pre-defined street corners across Los Santos, Blaine County, and Sandy Shores
  2. Approach NPCs — Walk up to pedestrians (not vehicles). Press E to initiate sale
  3. NPC Reaction — Random outcome:
    • Accept (60% chance): Sale completes, you receive payment
    • Reject (30% chance): NPC walks away, no transaction
    • Police (10% chance): NPC is undercover cop — instant +50 heat, possible arrest if backup arrives
  4. Heat Accumulation — Each successful sale adds +5 heat. Failed sales (reject/police) add +10-50 heat
  5. Cooldown — 30-second cooldown between sale attempts at the same location

4.3 Heat System (Street Selling)

Every street corner has an individual heat level (0-100) that increases with selling activity and decays over time when inactive.

Heat Level Police Response Decay Rate
0-25 (Safe) No alerts, normal patrols -1 heat/min
26-50 (Warm) Increased patrol frequency (+50%) -1 heat/min
51-75 (Hot) Dispatch alert to nearby units, undercover ops deployed -0.5 heat/min
76-100 (Burning) Active manhunt, K-9 units, surveillance helicopters -0.25 heat/min (very slow decay)
Automatic Arrest Warrant: If heat reaches 100 at any location, an arrest warrant is automatically issued with your last known description. Police can detain you on sight for 72 hours (in-game) or until you lay low and heat decays below 50.

4.4 Best Practices for Street Selling

  • Rotate Locations: Never sell at the same corner more than 3 times consecutively. Rotate between 4-5 locations to keep heat low
  • Watch the Clock: Sell during low patrol times (2am-6am in-game for LSPD zones, anytime for rural BCSO zones)
  • Use Lookouts: Gang members can act as lookouts (stand 50m away, report police in Discord/in-game chat)
  • Escape Routes: Pre-plan 2-3 escape routes from each selling zone (alleys, parking garages, off-road trails)
  • Burner Vehicle: Use a cheap, common vehicle (e.g., Asea, Ingot) instead of your personal car. Ditch it if pursued
  • No Gang Colors: Avoid wearing gang clothing while selling — it increases police suspicion and heat accumulation by 50%
  • Bribe Officers: If caught with low heat (under 30), offer $10,000 bribe (50% success rate, depends on officer's corruption level)

5. Territory Selling (Passive Income)

Territory selling integrates with the zo_territories gang control system, allowing you to set up passive drug distribution points within gang-controlled zones. This method generates income while you're offline or engaged in other activities, but requires gang membership and territorial defense.

5.1 How Territory Selling Works

  1. Capture Territory — Your gang must control a territory zone (see Gang SOP for capture mechanics)
  2. Set Up Drug Spot — Use /drugs setup at designated spot markers within your gang's territory (max 3 spots per territory)
  3. Stock Product — Transfer inventory from personal stash to the drug spot (up to 50 oz per spot)
  4. NPC Demand System — NPCs automatically purchase product every 15 minutes (in-game time) based on:
    • Product quality (high quality = faster sales)
    • Territory wealth level (Vinewood Hills buyers pay more than Strawberry)
    • Gang reputation (higher rep = more buyers)
  5. Collect Profits — Return to drug spot to collect cash. Uncollected profits are vulnerable to raids

5.3 Rival Raids

Enemy gangs can raid your drug spots to steal product and profits. Raids can occur when:

  • Your gang loses control of the territory (captured by rivals)
  • Enemy gang members locate your drug spot (50m proximity triggers discovery)
  • Rival gang initiates a deliberate raid using /gang raid command
Raid Defense: Gang members within 100m of a drug spot during a raid receive an alert. Successful defense (kill or repel raiders within 5 minutes) grants +50 gang reputation. Failed defense = loss of all product and profits at that spot.

Gang leaders can adjust profit-sharing percentage (5%-25% range) via /gang settings.

6. Police Heat System

The heat system is the primary law enforcement deterrent in ZO Syndicate RP. Heat accumulates from illegal activities and triggers escalating police responses. Understanding heat mechanics is critical to avoiding arrest and asset seizure.

6.1 Heat Accumulation

Activity Heat Gained Notes
Successful Street Sale +5 Per transaction, location-specific

Undercover Cop Sale +50 Instant alert, description logged
Drug Lab Explosion +30 Meth lab failures trigger neighbor reports

Selling in High-Patrol Zone (75+ heat) +10/sale Reckless behavior penalty

6.2 Heat Decay

Heat decays passively when you avoid illegal activity in that zone:

  • 0-50 Heat: -1 heat/min (60 heat/hour decay)
  • 51-75 Heat: -0.5 heat/min (30 heat/hour decay)
  • 76-100 Heat: -0.25 heat/min (15 heat/hour decay)
Accelerated Decay: Laying low (not logging in or staying outside the city) for 24 real-world hours reduces heat by -50 globally across all zones.

When heat reaches 75+, police dispatch receives automatic alerts:

Player description is based on:

  • Clothing: Top color, bottom color, hat/mask status
  • Vehicle: Make, model, color (if used for getaway)
  • Gang Affiliation: If wearing gang colors, dispatch identifies gang name

6.6 Drug Tests

Police can administer field drug tests to players:

  • Positive Result: Proves recent drug handling (60 min window after last production/sale)
  • Negative Result: No drug contact (clean for 60+ minutes)
  • Court Admissibility: Drug tests alone cannot convict, but combined with possession/witness testimony = strong case

6.7 Search Warrants

Judges can issue search warrants for drug operations if police provide:

  • Heat level 75+ in specific zone for 6+ hours
  • Witness testimony (NPC or undercover officer)
  • Surveillance evidence (player seen entering/exiting known lab location)
Warrant Execution: Police can raid your property, stash houses, and gang territories with a valid warrant. All product, equipment, and cash found are seized as evidence and forfeited to the state. Warrant raids also grant +100 heat, making you a priority target for 48 hours.

6.8 Penalties

Plea Bargains: If arrested with heat under 60 and no prior convictions, you can negotiate reduced charges with the DA in exchange for informant work (provide info on rival gangs, locations, etc.). Successful plea bargain: -50% jail time, -30% fine, no asset seizure.

6.9 How to Reduce Heat

  • Lay Low: Stop selling in high-heat zones for 2-4 hours (real-time) to allow natural decay
  • Change Appearance: Switch clothing, vehicle, and hairstyle to reset description in dispatch system
  • Bribe Police: $10,000 bribe (50% success rate) reduces heat by -30 if caught under 50 heat
  • Gang Intimidation: High-reputation gangs can threaten witnesses (removes NPC testimony, -20 heat)
  • Leave the City: Heat only applies to specific zones. Selling in a new city zone starts at 0 heat
  • Use Proxies: Pay recruit-level gang members to sell for you (they take the heat, you take 70% profit)

7. Gang Integration

Gang membership unlocks critical drug dealing infrastructure: secure stash houses, bulk discounts, profit sharing, and territorial control. Solo drug dealers face significantly higher risks and lower profit margins compared to gang-backed operations.

7.1 Gang Stash Houses

Each gang controls 2-4 stash houses (fortified safehouses) that provide:

  • Secure Storage: 500 oz capacity (compared to 50 oz personal inventory)
  • Quality Preservation: Humidity-controlled storage prevents quality degradation over time
  • Drying Racks: Faster weed drying (1 hour vs 2 hours) with +5% quality bonus
  • Equipment Storage: Store lab equipment, packaging supplies, and cash reserves
  • Raid Protection: Reinforced doors require 5+ police units + battering ram to breach (10 min breach time)
Gang Rank Access: Only Member rank and above can access stash houses. Recruits must store product in personal vehicles (vulnerable to theft/search).

7.2 Bulk Discounts

Gang reputation unlocks bulk purchasing discounts from black market suppliers:

Gang Reputation Discount Rate Unlocked Suppliers
0-250 (New Gang) No discount Street-level dealers only
251-500 (Established) 10% off ingredients/seeds Mid-tier suppliers (cartel contacts)
501-750 (Respected) 20% off ingredients/seeds Premium suppliers, lab equipment vendors
751-1000 (Dominant) 30% off all purchases Exclusive cartel connections, wholesale pricing

7.3 Rank Locks

Drug production access is gated by gang rank to prevent new recruits from blowing up meth labs or attracting police heat:

Gang Rank Drug Access Other Privileges
Recruit Weed only (grow, harvest, sell) Cannot access stash houses or gang vehicles
Member Weed + Cocaine (processing, selling) Stash house access, gang vehicle use
Enforcer All drugs (weed, cocaine, meth) Territory drug spot setup, raid leadership
Lieutenant All drugs + lab management Can upgrade labs, authorize bulk buys
Boss/Underboss Full control + profit oversight Access to all gang finances, supplier negotiations

7.4 Profit Sharing

Gang drug operations use a tiered profit-sharing model:

  • 10% to Gang Bank: Automatic deduction from all sales (street + territory)
  • 90% to Dealer: Remaining profit goes to the individual dealer
  • Boss Override: Gang leaders can adjust split (5%-25% to gang, 75%-95% to dealer) via gang settings

Example: You sell $10,000 worth of cocaine. $1,000 (10%) goes to gang bank, $9,000 (90%) is your profit.

Gang Bank Uses

  • Territory Defenses: Purchase turrets ($15,000), cameras ($5,000), reinforced stash houses ($25,000)
  • Vehicle Upgrades: Armor, engine tuning, bullet-resistant glass for gang vehicles
  • War Funds: Purchase heavy weapons (AK-47s, RPGs) for gang wars
  • Member Bonuses: Leaders can distribute bonuses for successful raids or territory captures

7.5 Gang Wars Over Drug Routes

Rival gangs can initiate wars to capture drug-rich territories:

  • War Declaration: Gang leader declares war on rival gang holding desired territory
  • Capture Objective: Control territory flag for 20 continuous minutes while defending against rivals
  • Drug Spot Control: Winning gang takes control of all drug spots in the territory (existing product is seized)
  • 24-Hour Immunity: After capture, territory cannot be challenged for 24 hours
War Consequences: Losing a drug-rich territory like Vespucci Beach can cost your gang $50,000-100,000/day in lost passive income. Territory defense is a top priority for profitable gangs.

7.6 Territory Control Benefits

Controlling drug-friendly territories grants bonuses beyond passive income:

  • +25% Profit Bonus: All sales in your gang's territory (street + passive) earn +25%
  • -20% Heat Accumulation: Police prioritize rival gangs, giving your members more operating room
  • Priority Supplier Access: Cartel contacts offer exclusive deals to territorial controllers (rare ingredients, discounted lab equipment)
  • Safe Zones: Stash houses in controlled territories have +50% raid resistance (10 police units required vs 5)

9. Getting Started Guide

New to the narcotics trade? Follow this 10-step progression path to build a sustainable drug empire while minimizing early-game risks.

Step 1: Join a Gang (Strongly Recommended)

While technically optional, gang membership provides critical infrastructure (stash houses, bulk discounts, backup during raids) that solo dealers lack. Apply to gangs recruiting in Discord or in-game at gang hangouts (Grove Street, Vespucci Beach clubhouse, etc.). Expect a 1-2 week probation period as a Recruit.

Step 2: Start with Weed (Low Risk, Learn Mechanics)

Purchase seed packs ($50) from black market dealers (marked on map with green leaf icon). Start with 10 seeds ($500 investment) to learn the grow cycle without risking major capital.

Step 3: Find a Grow House Location

Use /drugs map to reveal 10 grow house locations. Beginners should choose rural locations (Grapeseed, Harmony) to avoid police patrols. Each location has 6 planter slots.

Step 4: Plant Seeds, Water Regularly

Press E at planter to plant seed. Plants require watering every 4 hours (in-game time). Set real-world alarms or use Discord bot reminders. Missed watering = -10% quality per missed cycle.

Step 5: Harvest and Dry

After 24 hours growth, harvest plants (press E). Each plant yields 4-6 oz. Transport to drying location (gang stash house = 1 hour dry; personal property = 2 hours). Do not rush dry or quality drops to "Low."

Step 6: Sell on Street Corners (Learn Heat Management)

Take 10 oz to a low-risk selling zone (Grapeseed, Harmony, Paleto Bay). Press E on pedestrians to initiate sales. Watch heat meter (top-right HUD). Stop selling when heat reaches 30, switch locations. Track profit and learn which zones have best buyer acceptance rates.

Step 7: Progress to Cocaine Once Comfortable

After 1 week of weed operations and achieving Gang Member rank, purchase coca leaves ($120/kg) from cartel contacts. Use cocaine lab locations (revealed via /drugs map at Member rank). Complete processing minigame (match chemical ratios shown on screen). Start with 5 kg batches to learn mechanics.

Step 8: Set Up Territory Spots for Passive Income

Once your gang controls a territory, use /drugs setup at marked drug spot locations (max 3 per territory). Stock each spot with 30 oz of mid-quality cocaine. Check back every 6 hours to collect profits and restock. Passive income allows you to diversify activities (participate in gang wars, heists, legal jobs for cover).

Step 9: Invest in Gang Stash House

Contribute $10,000 to gang bank to unlock stash house privileges. Store product in bulk (500 oz capacity), use drying racks for faster processing, and keep cash reserves safe from police searches during traffic stops.

Step 10: Master Meth Cooking for Max Profit

After 2 weeks active play and Lieutenant rank, access meth labs. Practice cooking in offline mode (solo sessions) to avoid wasting $400 ingredient batches. Once you achieve 80%+ success rate, begin full-scale meth production. High-quality meth ($1,000/oz) in premium zones (Vinewood Hills) yields peak income but attracts maximum police attention. Always have $10,000+ emergency funds for bail and lawyer fees.

Beginner Mistake to Avoid: Do NOT wear gang colors while selling during your first month. Gang-affiliated dealers gain +3 heat per sale, drastically increasing arrest risk before you've learned heat management. Build income first, represent your gang later.

11. Tips & Best Practices

11.1 General Strategy

  • Always Check Heat Before Selling: Use /drugs heat before every session. Never sell in zones with 60+ heat.
  • Diversify Selling Locations: Rotate between 4-5 zones per session. Never return to same location twice in a row.
  • Build Relationships with Gang Members: Experienced dealers often mentor new recruits. Ask for tips on best selling times, lab shortcuts, and police patrol patterns.
  • Watch for Police Patrols: LSPD/BCSO patrol routes are semi-randomized but follow patterns. Learn peak patrol times (6am-10am, 6pm-10pm in-game) and avoid selling during those windows in high-risk zones.
  • Use Burner Vehicles: Purchase a cheap car ($5,000-10,000) for drug runs. If impounded/seized, the loss is minimal compared to your $100,000 personal vehicle.
  • Carry Minimal Product: Only carry what you plan to sell in that session (10-20 oz max). Store bulk product in gang stash to avoid losing everything in a single arrest.

11.2 Heat Management

  • Lay Low When Heat Hits 50: Stop selling, switch zones, or go offline for 1 hour (real-time) to allow decay.
  • Change Appearance After Close Calls: If you escape a police encounter, immediately change clothes, hairstyle, and vehicle to reset dispatch description.
  • Bribe Early, Not Late: Bribes have 50% success rate under 30 heat, 10% success rate over 60 heat. Offer bribes early during traffic stops.
  • Use Territory Bonus: Selling in your gang's territory reduces heat accumulation by -20%. Prioritize controlled zones.
  • Watch for Undercover Ops: When heat reaches 51+, dispatch deploys undercover units. NPCs in business attire (suits, ties) in low-income areas are likely cops. Avoid.

11.3 Production Efficiency

  • Set Watering Alarms: Use phone alarms or Discord bot notifications for every 4 hours (in-game time conversion: 4 in-game hours = 8 real-world minutes).
  • Batch Processing: Process cocaine in batches of 10 kg to minimize lab trips. Each trip adds +2 heat (suspicious activity).
  • Partner Cooking (Meth): Coordinate with another Lieutenant+ gang member for partner meth cooks (+10% quality, shared costs).
  • Invest in Lab Upgrades: $50,000 upgraded meth lab reduces failure rate from 30% to 10%, pays for itself after ~15 successful cooks.
  • Use Gang Stash Drying Racks: 50% faster drying + 5% quality bonus saves 1 hour per batch (critical for high-volume operations).

11.4 Financial Management

  • Maintain $20,000 Emergency Fund: Always keep $20,000 liquid for bail ($10,000) and lawyer fees ($5,000-10,000). Store in gang bank if you don't trust yourself to avoid impulse spending.
  • Reinvest 40% of Profits: Use 40% of weekly profits to buy better ingredients, upgrade labs, or expand to new territories. 60% is personal income.
  • Track Costs: Use in-game notepad or Discord channel to log ingredient costs, fines, bribes, and losses. Calculate actual net profit (many dealers overestimate earnings).
  • Diversify Income Streams: Don't rely 100% on drugs. Combine with legal job (mechanic, trucker) for cover story and steady baseline income during heat cooldowns.

11.5 Avoiding Arrest

  • Never Consent to Searches: Police need probable cause or warrant to search you/vehicle. Refuse consent (in RP: "I do not consent to searches"). Forces them to get warrant (buys you time).
  • Know Your Rights: You can remain silent during questioning. Do NOT admit to drug activity, even jokingly. Anything said is used in court (DOJ prosecutes based on RP interactions).
  • Hire a Lawyer Immediately: Contact a lawyer within 5 minutes of arrest. Good lawyers negotiate plea bargains (-50% jail time) or get charges dropped on technicalities.
  • Use the "/lawyer" Command: Request lawyer during booking process. Public defenders are free but less effective (30% case win rate vs 70% for private attorneys).
  • Record Police Interactions: Use in-game "Record" feature (if available) during stops. Evidence of police rights violations can get charges dismissed.

11.6 Gang Warfare Survival

  • Travel in Pairs During Wars: When gang wars are active, never sell solo. Partner with another gang member (one sells, one provides security).
  • Fortify Drug Spots: Spend gang funds on cameras ($5,000) and turrets ($15,000) at territory drug spots. Alerts you to raids 30 seconds before rivals arrive.
  • Evacuate Product During Raids: If raid alert triggers, immediately use /drugs collect to withdraw all product and cash. Store in gang stash ASAP.
  • Retaliate Strategically: After successful raid defense, coordinate counter-raid on enemy territory within 24 hours (gang morale boost + reputation gain).

13. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I get banned for drug dealing in ZO Syndicate RP?

A: No. Drug dealing is a fully supported game mechanic. However, you CAN be banned for:

  • Metagaming: Using out-of-character Discord info to avoid police (e.g., someone tells you "cops are at Grove Street" and you avoid it without in-character knowledge)
  • Fail RP: Breaking character, refusing to comply with valid police RP (e.g., ignoring /me commands, combat logging during arrest)
  • RDM (Random Deathmatch): Killing rival dealers without RP initiation

Q: What happens if I get caught by police?

A: Arrest process:

  1. Police detain you (cuff animation)
  2. Search for evidence (drugs, cash, weapons)
  3. Read Miranda rights (you have right to lawyer)
  4. Transport to MRPD (Mission Row Police Department)
  5. Booking process (mugshot, fingerprints, charges filed)
  6. Bail hearing (Judge sets bail: $5,000-50,000 depending on charges)
  7. Trial (if you plead not guilty) or sentencing (if guilty plea)
  8. Jail time (10-180 months depending on charges, 1 month = 1 minute real-time)

Q: How do I reduce police heat?

A: Six methods:

  • Natural Decay: Stop selling in that zone for 1-2 hours (real-time)
  • Change Appearance: New clothes, hair, vehicle = reset dispatch description
  • Bribe Officers: Offer $10,000 during traffic stop (50% success rate if heat under 30)
  • Lay Low: Don't log in for 24 hours = -50 heat globally
  • Gang Intimidation: High-rep gangs can threaten witnesses (removes NPC testimony, -20 heat)
  • Switch Zones: Heat is location-specific. Sell in new zone (starts at 0 heat)

Q: Can I sell in any gang's territory?

A: Street selling: Yes, but risky. Rival gangs may confront you (+robbery/death risk). Territory drug spots: No, only in your gang's controlled territory.

Q: What's the most profitable drug?

A: High-quality meth ($1,000/oz) in premium zones (Vinewood Hills, Vespucci Beach) yields highest income. However, profit-per-hour accounting for risk:

  • Weed: $1,800/hour (low risk, low skill)
  • Cocaine: $6,480/hour (medium risk, medium skill)
  • Meth: $7,200/hour (very high risk, high skill, explosion losses reduce effective rate)

Experienced dealers recommend cocaine for best risk-adjusted returns.

Q: How often do police raids happen?

A: Raid frequency depends on heat and gang activity:

  • Heat 0-50: Raids are extremely rare (under 5% chance per week)
  • Heat 51-75: Moderate raid risk (20% chance per week if pattern detected)
  • Heat 76-100: High raid risk (60% chance per week, active investigations)
  • Known Gang Territory: +30% raid risk modifier (police prioritize gang labs)

Q: Do I need to join a gang to sell drugs?

A: No, but highly recommended. Solo dealers miss out on:

  • Stash houses (secure storage, 500 oz capacity)
  • Bulk discounts (20-30% savings on ingredients)
  • Backup during raids (gang members defend your operations)
  • Territory passive income (extra $5,000-10,000/day)
  • Profit sharing benefits (gang upgrades benefit all members)

Q: Can police seize my personal vehicles?

A: Yes, if:

  • Vehicle used during drug sales (witness testimony or surveillance footage)
  • Drugs/cash found in vehicle during search
  • Vehicle registered to your name and you're convicted of trafficking charges

Protection: Use burner vehicles (cheap cars under $10,000) for drug runs. Register personal vehicles to fake IDs or gang shell companies (lawyer service, $5,000 fee).

Q: What happens if I die during a drug deal?

A: Standard death mechanics:

  • You drop all drugs and cash in inventory (lootable by killer or police)
  • EMS revives you at hospital (no memory of death per RP rules)
  • Heat level persists (death does NOT reset heat)
  • If police arrive before EMS, they can collect dropped product as evidence

Q: Can I grow/produce drugs in my personal apartment?

A: No. Drug production is restricted to designated grow houses and labs (revealed via /drugs map). Personal properties cannot be used to avoid exploits (unlimited safe production).

Q: How do I find a lawyer?

A: Three options:

  • In-Game Command: /lawyer during booking (requests public defender, free but 30% win rate)
  • Discord: Post in #legal-services channel, private attorneys respond (fees: $5,000-15,000, 70% win rate)
  • Gang Lawyer: Many gangs have retainer lawyers (included in gang dues, 60% win rate)

Q: Can I bribe judges to dismiss charges?

A: No. Judge bribery is prohibited (bannable offense). Bribes only work on patrol officers during initial stops (before charges are filed). Once in court, only lawyers can negotiate plea bargains.
